Sexual Orientation

Sexual orientation is separate from romantic orientation, it references the sexual feelings a person can or can't feel. A person's sexual and romantic orientations may be the same, or they may not be.
-sexual is a suffix and can be applied to most if not all terms that are also used to refer to romanticism, as -romantic is also a suffix.

Pansexual

Def: Not being limited in sexual choice in regard to biological sex, gender, or gender identity.
Flag: It has three horizontal bars that are pink, yellow and blue. The pink band symbolizes women, the blue men, and the yellow those of a non-binary gender, such as agender, bigender or genderfluid.

Demisexual

Def:A person who does not experience sexual attraction unless they form a strong emotional connection with someone. It's more commonly seen in but by no means confined to romantic relationships. The term demisexual comes from the orientation being "halfway between" sexual and asexual.
Flag:The flag meaning is very similar to the meaning for asexuality, although it differs in the appearance.

Bisexual

Def:Attracted to people of the same gender and an other gender, more commonly being defined as having a sexual attraction to people of at least two genders.
Flag:Pink represents the attraction to people of the same gender. Purple represents the overlapping attraction to multiple genders. Blue represents the attraction to people of the opposite gender.

Polysexual

Def:An identity where a person who is sexually attracted to many, but not all, genders or sexes.
Flag:Pink represents attraction to females. Green represents attraction to people with non binary gender identies. Blue represents attraction to males.

Asexual

Def:Someone who does not experience sexual attraction. Unlike celibacy, which people choose, asexuality is an intrinsic part of who someone is. There is considerable diversity among the asexual community; each asexual person experiences things like relationships, attraction, and arousal somewhat differently. Asexuality is distinct from celibacy or sexual abstinence, which are behaviours, while asexuality is considered to be a sexual orientation. Some asexuals do participate in sex, for a variety of reasons..
Flag:Black represents asexuality and the lack of sexual attraction. Grey represents grey-asexuality and demisexuality. White represents persons who don't identify as asexual, but are in relationships with asexual identifying people. The purple strip represents community and unity.
