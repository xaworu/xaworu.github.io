More

Definition:

Group of neurological disorders that causes a person to have recurrent seizures

Next

Symptoms

Seizures are the most visible symptom of epilepsy. However, the seizures and their effects on each individual may vary depending on the type of epilepsy that the person has. For example, it is possible for a seizure to go unnoticed. For other seizures, though, the general possible symptoms include:

  • Muscle twitching/spasms
  • Anxiety/fear
  • Mental confusion
  • Pins and needles sensation
  • Headache
  • Fainting

Types of Epilepsy

  • Idiopathic - no apparent cause
  • Cryptogenic - unidentified likely cause
  • Symptomatic - identified cause
  • Generalized - seizures involve the whole brain at once
  • Focal/partial - seizures start from one area of the brain

Treatment

The treatment for epilepsy varies on an individual basis, and the treatment prescribed depends on several factors such as the frequency and severity of the seizures, as well as the person's age, overall health, and medical history. Some of the common treatment options include:

  • Medication, most commonly anticonvulsant drugs
  • Surgery, only in certain cases
  • Vagus nerve stimulation
  • Ketogenic diet
  • Epilepsy pacemaker (still being researched)
