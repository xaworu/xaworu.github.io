Romantic Orientation

Romantic orientation is separate from sexual orientation, it references the romanticism a person can or can't feel. A person's romantic and sexual orientations may be the same, or they may not be.
-romantic is a suffix and can be applied to most if not all terms that also refer to sexuality, as -sexual is also a suffix.

Aromantic


Def: a person who experiences little or no romantic attraction to others. Where romantic people have an emotional need to be with another person in a romantic relationship, aromantics are often satisfied with friendships and other non-romantic relationships.
Flag: Green because its the opposite color of red and the common color for romance. Yellow stands for friendship due to yellow roses. Orange is between yellow and red that can relate to grey-romantic. Black is for those that reject the traditional idea of romantic.

Lithromantic


Def: Feeling a romantic attraction towards others and also enjoying romantic relationships in theory, but not needing or wanting that affection to be reciprocated or to be in a relationship. Lithromantic is also known as akoiromantic.

Demiromantic

Def:a type of grey-romantic who only experiences romantic attraction after developing an emotional connection beforehand. Demiromantics do not experience primary romantic attraction, but they are capable of secondary romantic attraction. The sexual counterpart to demiromantic is demisexual.
Flag:This is the romantic counterpart to demisexual, so the meaning is the same, but in referenceto romantic orientation.

Grey-aromantic

Def:a person with a romantic orientation that is somewhere between aromantic and romantic. For example, a gray-romantic may: Experience romantic attraction but not very often. Experience romantic attraction, but not desire romantic relationships.
Flag:This is the romantic counterpart to grey-asexual, so the flag meaning is the same, but in reference to romantic orientation.

Panromantic

Def:A person who is romantically attracted to others but is not limited by the other's sex or gender. Similar to biromantic. Panromantics will tend to feel that their partner's gender does little to define their relationship.
Flag:This is the romantic counterpart to pansexual, so the flag meaning is the same, but in reference to romantic orientation.

Biromantic

Def:A person who is romantically attracted to two sexes or genders.Biromantic asexuals seek romantic relationships for a variety of reasons including companionship, affection, and intimacy, but they are not sexually attracted to their romantic partners. The sexual counterpart to biromantic is bisexual.
