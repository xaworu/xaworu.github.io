APD

Auditory Processing Disoder

When a person has a difference in the way that they understand and retain spoken languages.

Scroll down for more information.

More

Effects

  • 1. Difficulty distancing sound from another
  • 2. Trouble memorizing spoken words
  • 3. Difficulty separating sounds in one word
  • 4. Easily distracted by noises
  • 5. Trouble with following spoken directions
  • 6. Ignores people when they are talking
  • 7. Trouble with figurative languages
  • 8. Difficulty understanding complex sentences
  • 9. Mispronounces words
  • 10. Trouble focusing in a lecture or speech
Next

Support

1. Speak slower

  • It helps when you speak slower because it allows them to process each word one at a time.

2. Look them in eye

  • This allows them to somewhat read your lips so they can understand what you are saying visually.

3. Give them a quiet place to word

  • This helps because there are no outside noise that could distract them and helps them focus when there is no noise.
Next

Tips

  • 1. Ask them to repeat directions back to you
  • 2. Keep them organized
  • 3. Show what you mean instead of explain
  • 4. Allow them to think before replying
  • 5. Provide the presentation before a big lecture or the teacher notes
